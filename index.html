Nelson

Celebrate 67 Minutes

Nelson Rolihlahla Mandela, a prominent figure in South African history, was an anti-apartheid activist, politician, and statesman. Serving as South Africa's inaugural president from 1994 to 1999, he marked a pivotal moment in the nation's history by becoming its first black head of state through a fully representative democratic process. His legacy continues to inspire people worldwide. Mandela Day urges individuals to dedicate 67 minutes, symbolizing each year of Mandela's service, to contribute to global humanitarian efforts, fostering positive change in their communities and beyond. It emphasizes the ongoing commitment to making the world a better place, not just on a designated day but every day.
